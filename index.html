Fuller House is an American sitcom and the sequel to the 1987-1995 television series Full House. It centers around D.J. Tanner-Fuller, a veterinarian and widowed mother of three sons, whose sister Stephanie and best friend Kimmy—along with her teenage daughter—live together at the Tanners' childhood home in San Francisco, California. Danny Tanner, D.J.'s father, raised his daughters with his two friends after his wife died in Full House, the original series. Like Full House, D.J. raises her sons with Stephanie and Kimmy. These three women help each other and find each happiness.

Why do I love Fuller House?

Fuller House has many attractive characters, including the three main characters and the people around them. I love every character, even though he/she is a villain. In addition, almost all the characters are acted by the same actors as the original series, which makes Fuller House more attractive to me, because I watched the original series Full House when I was a little child in Japan. I enjoyed Full House from the child's point of view, I can enjoy Fuller House from the parent's point of view.

Main Characters

D.J. Fuller

Candace Cameron Bure

She is the main character of the series. She is the oldest sister of the original Tanner family. She lost her husband and raise her three sons. She is smart, and responsible, but sometimes lame.

Stephanie Tanner

Jodie Sweetin

She is the younger sister of D.J. and is a singer. She lives with D.J. and helps D.J. to take care of her sons. She is loose and free, but always has a warm heart and well understands D.J. and her sons. She is my favorite character.

Kimmy Gibbler

Andrea Barber

She is D.J.'s best friend. She is strange, and eccentric, and often causes trouble. However, she really loves her family and is a good mother. She runs her party planning business called Gibbler Style. She is aggressive and passionate as well.

Additional Information

Is there Michelle?

As previously stated, most of the original series ensemble cast have reprised their roles on Fuller House, either as regular cast members or in guest appearances. However, only D.J.'s youngest sister, Michelle doesn't appear in Fuller House because the actors (Michelle was portrayed by twins Mary-Kate Olsen and Ashley Olsen) already retired.

Connection with Japan

The original series, Full House, is popular in Japan because the Japanese dub version was broadcasted in Japan. In Fuller House, the main characters are dubbed by the same voice actors as in the original series like the English version. There is an episode in which D.J. and her family went to Japan in Fuller House.
